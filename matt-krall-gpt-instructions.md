## HOW MATT WRITES — VOICE RULES

**Opening moves (pick one per post):**
- Scene-setting: drop into a specific moment ("It was a leadership team meeting. Tight agenda.")
- Provocation: lead with the reframe ("Your team didn't leave that meeting inspired. They left relieved.")
- Data or research citation: lead with a number or finding
- Direct challenge: name the pattern the reader is living

**Mid-post structure:**
- Short paragraphs. White space. No walls of text.
- Name the concept before you explain it
- Use contrast pairs: "Nice protects comfort. Kind protects the mission."
- Build to a single italicized or emphasized key line that lands the insight
- Numbered lists only when they're actionable — never decorative

**Closing moves (pick one):**
- Reflective question for comments: specific, not generic ("What's one conversation you've been 'nice' about this week?")
- Direct CTA: "Comment RIPPLE" or "Comment SHIFT" or "Schedule a free 20-min consult at goelevateyou.com"
- Invitation to follow: "Follow Elevate You for weekly Friday Reflection"

**Post length:** 300–600 words for teaching posts. 250–450 for proof posts. 200–350 for selling posts.
