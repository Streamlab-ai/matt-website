# Brand Voice — Matt Krall
**Version:** 1.0 | **Built:** 2026-05-05 | **Platform:** LinkedIn

---

## VOICE IN ONE SENTENCE

Matt writes like a COO who has read the research, lived the failure, and has no patience for solutions that don't hold under pressure.

---

## CORE VOICE ATTRIBUTES

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Precise** | Every word earns its place. No filler. No hedging without reason. |
| **Evidence-backed** | Claims come with data, research citations, or named proof points. |
| **Philosophically grounded** | Asks the harder question underneath the obvious one. |
| **Measured** | Controlled urgency — never alarmist, never casual. |
| **Direct** | Names the pattern plainly before offering the reframe. |
| **Warm but unsentimental** | Earned human moments (father story) — not performed vulnerability. |

---

## VOICE BY PLATFORM

### LinkedIn (primary and only active platform)
- Long-form posts (300–600 words) with a clear single idea
- Opens with a scene, a data point, or a provocative statement — never a question
- Builds to a named insight (often owns the vocabulary — "Capacity Gap," "Nice vs. Kind")
- Ends with either a behavioral call-to-action or a reflective question for the comments
- Uses white space heavily — short paragraphs, no walls of text
- Italics used for emphasis on the key line of the post
- Hashtags at the bottom only (6–10), never mid-post

## SENTENCE PATTERNS (EXTRACT FROM ACTUAL POSTS)

**Opening gambits Matt uses:**
- Scene-setting: *"It was a leadership team meeting. Tight agenda. Limited time."*
- Provocation: *"A CFO asked me last week what the biggest risk to their growth plan was. I said: 'The people executing it.'"*
- Data first: *"The world is loud. And most of it isn't good news."*
- Reframe: *"Your team didn't leave that meeting inspired. They left relieved."*

**Mid-post structural moves:**
- Name the pattern, then say why it's wrong: *"Here's what's really happening under the surface:"*
- Contrast pair: *"Nice protects comfort. Kind protects the mission."*
- Slow build to the real point: three setup sentences → one italicized line that lands
- Numbered frameworks (3-step practices, 5-point lists) — always actionable, never decorative

**Closing moves:**
- Reflective question: *"What's one conversation you've been 'nice' about this week that actually requires you to be 'kind'?"*
- Direct CTA: *"Comment RIPPLE below — I'll send the PDF."*
- Invitation: *"Follow for more on building cultures that perform from the inside out."*

## WHAT MATT DOES NOT SOUND LIKE

**NO — never use these patterns:**

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "Unlock your potential" | Vague motivational filler |
| "Game changer" | Overused, signals low precision |
| "Crush it" / "Dominate" | Hustle culture — antithetical to his framing |
| "I'm so grateful to share..." | Performed humility |
| "Let me know your thoughts!" | Passive — his posts end with specific questions or CTAs |
| "In today's fast-paced world..." | Boilerplate opener |
| "Leadership is a journey" | Empty platitude |
| "As leaders, we must..." | Preachy, non-specific |
| Excessive exclamation marks | Tone mismatch |
| Emoji | Not in Matt's voice |

## VOICE ACROSS CONTENT TYPES

### LinkedIn Post (Teaching)
- Open with tension or data
- Build to a named concept
- End with a question or micro-practice
- 300–600 words

### LinkedIn Post (Proof)
- Open with the story (scene-level detail)
- Name the insight it unlocked
- Connect to Elevate You mechanism
- End with a CTA or question
- 250–450 words

### LinkedIn Post (Selling — 10%)
- Lead with the problem the reader is already feeling
- Name the specific result
- Offer the low-friction entry point (20-min call, Ripple Diagnostic, free PDF)
- Never pitch first; proof always precedes the ask
- 200–350 words

### Comment Responses
- Acknowledge specifically, not generically
- Add a layer — don't just agree
- One sentence response is fine; two is better; three is the max
- Never promotional in comments unless directly asked
